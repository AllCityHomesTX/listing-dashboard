Why Online Estimates Vary — And What They Can't See

🤖
Texas Doesn't Share Sale Prices Publicly
Texas is a "non-disclosure state" — actual sale prices are only recorded through the MLS, not filed with the county. Zillow and Trulia can't access this data, so their algorithms are working with educated guesses. This is the single biggest reason Texas estimates are less reliable than in most other states.
📸
Algorithms Can't Walk Through the Front Door
No algorithm can see the greenbelt backing, mature trees, clean interior, or quality of the patio on this home. Estimates are built from tax records and square footage alone. The features buyers actually respond to — the ones that showed up in showing feedback — are invisible to Zillow.
📅
Estimates Lag. Markets Don't.
Zillow acknowledges a median error of 1.83% for active listings — roughly ±$5,000 on this home. Neighborhood prices in Burning Wood have shifted 11.5% over two years. Algorithms are slow to catch up to local shifts that a licensed agent sees in real time through the MLS.
📖
Zillow's Own Track Record Tells the Story
In 2016, Zillow's co-founder sold his Seattle home for 40% below its own Zestimate. In 2021, Zillow shut down its home-buying program after losing $881M relying on its own algorithm to price homes. These aren't criticisms — they're Zillow's own public record.

Common Questions About These Numbers

Questions buyers often ask — and the full context behind each answer.
"The Zillow estimate is below list — does that mean it's overpriced?" +
No. The $4,900 gap is well within Zillow's own published error range of 1.83% for active listings — which on this home is ±$5,000. Texas also doesn't publicly file sale prices, so Zillow's algorithm is making educated estimates from incomplete data. The list price was set using actual SABOR MLS sold data that Zillow simply doesn't have.
"Why is the Zillow range so wide — $240K to $294K?" +
A wide range is Zillow's way of flagging uncertainty — the algorithm is telling you it doesn't have enough data to be confident. In Texas, that's almost always the non-disclosure issue. A $54,000 spread from a single estimate is a signal to trust local expertise, not the algorithm.
"Redfin seems close to list price — is that a better signal?" +
Redfin does have MLS access in Texas, which gives it a modest data advantage over Zillow. Their own 78247 statistics show homes typically sell around 2% below list price in about 51 days. At $272,000, a 2% discount equals ~$266,560 — a fair negotiation range that's already baked into the pricing strategy.
"Trulia shows the same number as Zillow — are those independent opinions?" +
No — Zillow acquired Trulia in 2015. Both sites run on the same algorithm from the same data. When you see them match, you're looking at one source presented as two. Redfin is the only genuinely independent estimate in the big three.
